The Ketogenic Diet

Life in Ketosis

What is the Keto Diet?

The ketogenic diet is an eating pattern that includes high amounts of fat, low to moderate amounts of protein, and very little carbohydrates. The keto diet is typically rich in foods like butter, cheese, eggs, meat, nuts, oils, seafood, and seeds. It does not allow much, if any, room for fruits, vegetables, grains, potatoes, sweets, or other carbohydrate-rich foods. By restricting carbohydrates and monitoring protein intake, a ketogenic diet aims to put your body in a state of nutritional ketosis, a metabolic process that increases the production of ketones, which are made by the liver and can be used as the body’s primary energy source. When we are not in ketosis, glucose (derived from carbohydrate intake) is our body’s primary energy source.

    Pros:

  • Studies suggest that while in ketosis, subjects reported a decrease in hunger.
  • The ketogenic diet excludes sugar, many carbohydrates, and most processed foods.
  • A ketogenic diet has even been shown to reduce seizures in pediatric patients with epilepsy.

    Cons:

  • Due to high fat consumption, there are potential risks to the health of one's heart.
  • The keto diet's benefits can be negated if not properly followed. Many everday foods fall outside of the accepted diet plan and can interrupt ketosis.
  • Although a reduction of sugar intake can be great, we still require some sugar in our diets so that our bodies function properly.

Common food-related allergies to look out for while on a keto diet include dairy, eggs, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat and soy. It's still entirely possible to maintain a keto diet even with these allergies, as there are many other food alternatives!
